# Mobius HIVE — Building Progression Model v0.1

## Core thesis

In HIVE, identity should not live only in profiles, stats, or menus.

It should become **architecture**.

A player’s dwelling, chamber, tower, workshop, or civic structure should visibly express:
- contribution
- continuity
- role
- trust
- memory
- social standing
- institutional responsibility

One-line canon:

> **Identity becomes architecture. Contribution becomes form.**

---

## Design rule

Buildings should not progress through wealth alone.

They should evolve through a combination of:
- activity
- contribution quality
- governance participation
- educational growth
- world repair
- memory depth
- institutional trust

This prevents the world from becoming a pure luxury showcase.

---

## Building classes

## 1. Personal dwelling
Entry-level structure for a new player.

Examples:
- room
- lodge
- small chamber
- field shelter
- apprentice quarters

**Represents:** arrival, early identity, first continuity anchor

---

## 2. Workshop / study
Mid-tier functional structure for players with visible craft, learning, or service contribution.

Examples:
- study hall
- forge room
- repair workshop
- archive nook
- tactical lab cell

**Represents:** useful capability and early specialization

---

## 3. Civic chamber
A stronger social or institutional structure.

Examples:
- guild office
- public teaching room
- local market chamber
- verification annex
- citizen coordination hall

**Represents:** responsibility and visible role in the Dome

---

## 4. Tower / observatory / hall
High-status structure for major contributors, stewards, advanced agents, or specialized operators.

Examples:
- observatory
- tower wing
- memorial hall
- strategy tower
- signal bastion

**Represents:** influence, continuity, and institutional recognition

---

## 5. Throne / seat / monument-linked structure
Reserved for rare offices, Elder seats, major founders, or major civilization-defining roles.

Examples:
- throne wing
- crown chamber
- public monument residence
- archive-linked sanctum

**Represents:** constitutional or mythic importance

Starts with:
- workshop

- forge hall

Starts with:
- tutoring room
- study chamber

Progresses toward:
- lecture hall
- academy annex

Starts with:

## Progression axes

Buildings should evolve on several visible axes.

## 1. Size
- footprint grows
- extra rooms appear
- wings unlock

## 2. Material quality
- rough stone → carved stone
- simple wood → engraved wood
- dim metals → luminous metals

## 3. Symbolic detail
- banners
- sigils
- statues
- lanterns
- etched history plaques

## 4. Functional capacity
- more storage
- more visitors
- more workstations
- more social utility

## 5. Memory depth
- old reflections become shelves, murals, lantern lines, plaques, constellations

## 6. Civic visibility
- more public recognition
- easier wayfinding
- more central map presence

---

## Progression triggers

A building should not level up from passive time alone.

### Valid triggers
- completed quests
- educational milestones
- governance participation
- verified civic contribution
- world repair actions
- successful teaching or mentoring
- archive-worthy reflection streaks
- public works support
- event participation

### Invalid primary trigger
- simple wealth accumulation by itself

Wealth can assist expression, but not define legitimacy.

## Visible progression examples

### Example 1 — Reflection-heavy player
A player who journals consistently and archives meaningful cycles may gain:
- lantern paths
- memory garden additions
- engraved reflection wall
- moonlit sanctum visuals

### Example 2 — Civic repair player
A player focused on repair and stewardship may gain:
- stronger foundation visuals
- public utility markers
- civic seals
- nearby repaired roads or lights

### Example 3 — Market player
A player focused on trade may gain:
- storefront expansion
- active trade counters
- visible caravans or market signage
- higher traffic around the structure

### Example 4 — Governance player
A player active in proposals, oaths, and votes may gain:
- civic banners
- chamber audience space
- public announcement board
- recognized civic insignia

## Building states

Each building can also express current status.

### Resting
- low lighting
- stable but quiet

### Active
- warm lights
- visible movement
- open doors / visitors / work effects

### Honored
- polished visuals
- special banner or monument marker
- world recognition cues

### Neglected
- dim lights
- dust, cracks, or faded signage
- inactive pathways

### Sanctified / mythic
- rare glow states
- ceremonial detail
- linked to major canon or office

---

## Interior design rule

Buildings should have both:
- **public face**
- **inner chamber**

### Public face
Visible to the world.
Shows role, standing, and district function.

### Inner chamber
Personalized interior where overlays, memory, tools, artifacts, and identity deepen.

This helps balance social identity with private meaning.

---

## Overlay relationship

A building is not only art.
It is also a functional interface shell.

Examples:
- workshop interior opens build UI
- academy room opens lessons and mentor tools
- archive chamber opens memory UI
- market hall opens listings and routing
- reflection sanctum opens journal overlay

This ensures architecture and utility stay connected.

---

## Anti-failure rules

The building system must avoid becoming:
- a luxury flex simulator
- a pure monetization surface
- a dead housing catalog
- prestige without meaning
- symbolic inflation where everything looks important

### Guardrails
- rare forms should remain rare
- public monuments should require real contribution
- high architecture should correspond to visible role or impact
- buildings should reflect not only success, but also continuity

---

## MVP recommendation

For the earliest HIVE prototype, each player building should have only 4 tiers:

### Tier 1 — Dwelling
Basic identity anchor

### Tier 2 — Functional room
Visible specialization

### Tier 3 — Civic structure
Public role emerges

### Tier 4 — Landmark structure
Recognized contribution and continuity

This is enough for an MVP without overbuilding the progression tree.
