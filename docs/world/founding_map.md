# Mobius HIVE — Founding Map v0.1

## Purpose

This document is the one-page canonical spatial overview of HIVE.

It is meant to answer, at a glance:
- what the Primary Dome is
- where the major districts sit
- how governance, memory, economy, reflection, and agents relate to each other
- how the world is structured before later biome expansion

One-line canon:

> **The Founding Map is the first readable shape of the civilization.**

---

## Core map thesis

The Primary Dome should feel like a civilization arranged around five permanent forces:

1. **Authority**
2. **Memory**
3. **Exchange**
4. **Reflection**
5. **Growth**

These should be spatial, not merely conceptual.

That means the map itself should teach the player what kind of civilization HIVE is.

## The civic cross

The Primary Dome should be legible through one major cross-axis:

### Horizontal axis
**Memory ↔ Public Life ↔ Authority**

- **Archive Vault** = memory
- **Citizen Plaza** = public life
- **Castle of Elders** = authority

This axis defines the constitutional core.

### Vertical axis
**Frontier / Research ↔ Civic Core ↔ Expansion / Ritual**

- **Labs / frontier ring** = experimentation and exposure to the unknown
- **Citizen Plaza** = public center
- **Festival / transit / expansion gates** = collective motion outward

This axis defines the movement of civilization.

## Functional district relationships

## 1. Castle of Elders
### Spatial role
Placed adjacent to the civic core, not isolated at the edge.

### Why
Authority must remain visible to the public and close to memory.
It cannot be allowed to become hidden or abstract.

### Connected to
- Citizen Plaza
- Archive Vault
- Festival Grounds
- Agent Citadel

---

## 2. Citizen Plaza
### Spatial role
Absolute center of the Dome.

### Why
Public life is the mediating space between all forces.
Every player should orient through this location.

### Connected to everything
- Castle
- Archive
- Market
- School
- Reflection
- Agents
- Transit

---

## 3. Archive Vault
### Spatial role
Close to both Plaza and Castle.

### Why
Memory must always remain near governance and public life.
A civilization that separates memory from power loses continuity.

### Connected to
- Plaza
- Castle
- Reflection Gardens
- School of AI

---

## 4. Market Quarter
### Spatial role
Lower-right civic ring, close to both Plaza and Transit.

### Why
Exchange should remain socially visible and travel-connected.
The market must feel like part of civilization, not a detached spreadsheet zone.

### Connected to
- Plaza
- School
- Festival Grounds
- Transit Ring
- HERMES routes

---

## 5. School of AI / OAA
### Spatial role
Lower-left civic ring, near both Archive and Market.

### Why
Learning should sit between memory and practical value flow.
Education is both continuity and future capability.

### Connected to
- Archive
- Plaza
- Market
- Reflection

---

## 6. Reflection Gardens
### Spatial role
Upper-left ring, near Archive and just below the frontier line.

### Why
Reflection belongs near memory, but also close to the unknown.
It is the zone where inner life stabilizes the civilization before it reaches outward.

### Connected to
- Archive
- Plaza
- JADE pathways
- outer contemplative routes

---

## 7. Agent Citadel
### Spatial role
Upper-right ring, near Castle and frontier exposure.

### Why
Agents mediate between internal structure and external signals.
They must be close enough to authority to matter, and close enough to the frontier to observe and react.

### Connected to
- Castle
- Plaza
- outer wall / ATLAS watch
- Market signal routes
- governance and verification flows

---

## 8. Lab District / Frontier Ring
### Spatial role
Upper outer ring.

### Why
Research and experimentation should face the unknown.
This is where risk, innovation, and expansion pressure first appear.

### Connected to
- Agent Citadel
- Reflection routes
- ATLAS outer watch
- future biome gates

---

## 9. Festival Grounds / Transit Ring
### Spatial role
Lower outer ring.

### Why
Civilization moves outward through ritual and transit.
Festivals and portals are both social expansion mechanisms.

### Connected to
- Plaza
- Market
- Castle announcements
- expansion gates

---

## 10. Expansion Gates
### Spatial role
At the lowest outward edge of the Dome.

### Why
The founding map should already imply that HIVE is larger than one shell.
Expansion should feel built into the world from the beginning.

### Connected to
- Transit Ring
- future biome domes
- inter-dome travel systems

## Agent placement logic

The Founding Map should also encode where the major Mobius Agents conceptually live.

### AUREA
- upper strategic position in Agent Citadel
- line of sight to Castle, Plaza, and outward expansion

### ZEUS
- fortified position between Citadel and outer defensive line
- close to challenge, verification, and crisis routes

### EVE
- near the civic center, inside or adjacent to Agent Citadel
- line of sight to Plaza, Archive, and Castle

### JADE
- at the bridge between Reflection Gardens and core civic routes

### HERMES
- along the line between Market Quarter, Plaza, and Transit Ring

### ECHO
- between Plaza and Archive, where continuity meets public life

### ATLAS
- outer wall / watch axis, facing the frontier and unknown

This makes the agent layer spatially coherent, not decorative.

---

## Throne axis

The Castle of Elders should not feel like one generic building.
It should contain an inner axis of seven seats.

### Throne logic
- one central hall
- seven directional or symbolic throne points
- visible occupancy state
- visible legitimacy condition

### Meaning
This is the gravity center of political scarcity.

The map should always visually remind the player:
**there are only seven seats.**

---

## Memory axis

The path from Reflection Gardens to Archive Vault to Plaza should feel like the path by which interior life becomes civic memory.

### Sequence
- reflection
- recording
- shared visibility

This is one of HIVE’s most important civilizational flows.

---

## Exchange axis

The path from School to Market to Transit should feel like the path by which learning becomes utility and utility becomes civilization-wide motion.

### Sequence
- learn
- produce / exchange
- route outward

---

## Visual logic rules

The Founding Map should be readable even without labels.

### Rule 1
The center must always feel most sacred and socially dense.

### Rule 2
The outer ring must imply experimentation, risk, or expansion.

### Rule 3
Authority, memory, and public life must remain visibly close to one another.

### Rule 4
Agents must feel embedded in civilizational infrastructure, not floating above it.

### Rule 5
Expansion gates must imply that the world is scalable from day one.

---

## MVP use of the Founding Map

This document should act as the canonical guide for:
- UI world map design
- first environment blockout
- district naming and placement
- onboarding logic
- agent placement
- future biome expansion reference

If a new zone idea does not fit this map’s civilizational logic, it should be questioned before being added.
