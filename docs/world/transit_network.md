# Mobius HIVE — Transit Network v0.1

## Purpose

This document defines how movement works across the Primary Dome and, later, across the larger HIVE civilization network.

Transit is not just convenience.
It is one of the ways the civilization explains itself.

A good transit system should make the player feel:
- where the civic center is
- where value flows
- where authority sits
- where danger begins
- where the world can expand next

One-line canon:

> **Transit is the visible circulation system of the civilization.**

---

## Core thesis

Movement in HIVE should happen in three modes:

### 1. Walk mode
For discovery, social presence, mood, and environmental reading.

### 2. Routed travel
For fast travel, productivity, and repeat access to known destinations.

### 3. Ritual movement
For processions, festivals, governance ceremonies, Elder challenges, and major civic moments.

These three modes together make movement feel human, useful, and meaningful.

---

## Primary Dome transit logic

The Dome should have a clear circulation structure.

## A. Civic center routes
The highest-density routes should connect:
- Citizen Plaza
- Castle of Elders
- Archive Vault

These are the constitutional arteries.

## B. Daily civilization routes
The next ring of movement should connect:
- Market Quarter
- School of AI / OAA
- Reflection Gardens
- Agent Citadel

These are the everyday life paths.

## C. Outer ring routes
The outer paths should connect:
- Lab District
- Festival Grounds
- Transit Ring
- Expansion Gates
- outer wall watch lines

These are the frontier and expansion paths.

---

## Transit layers

## Layer 1 — Ground routes
### Form
- roads
- bridges
- plazas
- garden paths
- civic walkways

### Purpose
- exploration
- social visibility
- reading the world at human speed

### Design rule
Important ground routes should reveal major landmarks as the player moves.

---

## Layer 2 — Portal / fast-travel nodes
### Form
- portal arches
- district gates
- transit spires
- signal circles
- ring lifts

### Purpose
- reduce repetition for returning players
- preserve usability without killing atmosphere

### Rule
Fast travel should be unlockable or at least context-aware.
The first experience of a place should usually be earned through physical approach.

---

## Layer 3 — Ritual routes
### Form
- ceremonial roads
- procession stairs
- oath walks
- challenge corridors
- festival parade paths

### Purpose
- make major civic events feel theatrical and public

### Rule
Ritual movement should feel slower, more deliberate, and more visible than normal travel.

### Civic life ring
- Plaza ↔ Market

- School ↔ Market

### Expansion routes
- Festival Grounds ↔ Transit Ring
- Transit Ring ↔ Expansion Gates

## Transit identities by district

Transit should not feel identical everywhere.

### Castle routes
- formal
- elevated
- symmetrical
- guarded

### Archive routes
- quieter
- engraved
- lantern-marked
- memory-rich

### Market routes
- busy
- layered
- noisy
- dense with side passages

### Reflection routes
- softer
- slower
- curved
- contemplative

### Agent Citadel routes
- signal-rich
- geometric
- institutional
- precise

### Lab/frontier routes
- more exposed
- experimental
- less ceremonially finished

This helps movement reinforce district personality.

---

## Travel states

The transit network should visually respond to world state.

### Nominal
- all major routes open
- warm path lighting
- stable portal availability

### Elevated
- alert markers on certain routes
- stronger signal overlays
- more visible guidance lines

### Critical
- some routes restricted
- defensive detours active
- warning bands near threat zones
- faster routing to civic response locations

### Festival / Renewal
- temporary processional paths open
- special lights, banners, and crowd motion
- event-only destination nodes appear

## Inter-dome transit

As HIVE expands, transit must scale beyond the Primary Dome.

## Phase 1 — intra-dome only
Movement remains inside one world shell.

## Phase 2 — experimental outer gates
Players can see, but not fully access, future biome gates.

## Phase 3 — active inter-dome portals
Players can travel between Domes with clear transition rituals.

## Phase 4 — civilization transit network
The HIVE becomes a network of worlds connected by:
- portal rings
- rails
- airships
- tideways
- orbital lifts
- shard gates

---

## Travel meaning across worlds

Inter-dome movement should not feel like clicking tabs.
It should feel like:
- expedition
- transfer
- diplomatic visit
- pilgrimage
- assignment
- migration

This preserves civilizational weight.

---

## Player UX rules

## Rule 1
A new player should never be confused about where to go next.

## Rule 2
A returning player should never feel forced to walk too much for basic utility.

## Rule 3
Major places should be reachable fast, but first discovery should still matter.

## Rule 4
Movement should teach the structure of the world.

## Rule 5
Civic rituals should always have their own movement language.

---

## MVP recommendation

For the first playable HIVE version, implement:
- walkable roads between five priority districts
- minimap routing
- one fast-travel system
- one visible ritual/procession route
- one inactive or teaser expansion gate

This is enough to prove that movement itself helps explain the civilization.

---

## Anti-failure rules

Transit must avoid becoming:
- empty traversal padding
- teleport menu spam
- purely decorative roads
- content corridors with no civilizational logic
- disconnected biomes that feel like separate games

### Guardrails
- every major route should communicate purpose
- movement should reveal landmarks and hierarchy
- fast travel should help usability without erasing place
- ritual travel should remain special
