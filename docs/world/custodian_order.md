# HIVE — Custodian Order

## Purpose

This document defines the Custodian systems of HIVE.

Custodians are not background NPCs.

They are:
- the immune system of the Dome
- preservers of continuity
- maintainers of civilization integrity
- protectors of memory.

One-line canon:

> Custodians are the reason the civilization survives after the festivals end.

---

# Custodian Classes

## Janitors

Maintain public zones.

Responsibilities:
- clean corruption
- repair damaged structures
- stabilize mirror fractures
- restore festival grounds.

Janitor labor directly increases:
- biome stability
- morale
- Global Integrity.

---

## Archivists

Protect civilization memory.

Responsibilities:
- preserve canon
- record Elder succession
- seal historical events
- maintain memory continuity.

Without Archivists:
HIVE loses historical truth.

---

## Repairers

Infrastructure engineers of the Dome.

Responsibilities:
- maintain transit systems
- stabilize shard cores
- reconnect biomes
- repair orbital systems.

Late-game Repairers become civilization-critical.

---

## Witnesses

Validators of reality continuity.

Responsibilities:
- verify Trials of Mirrors
- attest throne transfers
- record catastrophes
- confirm legitimacy.

Witnesses cannot be bribed.

Their role is sacred.

---

## Silent Custodians

High-order stabilizers.

Rules:
- cannot become Elders
- cannot control economies
- cannot lead factions.

They serve the Dome itself.

They appear during:
- collapses
- civil wars
- biome failures
- corruption events.

---

# The First Custodian

A mythic figure.

Possible revelation:

The HIVE was not originally built as a game.

It was built to preserve civilization continuity after systemic collapse.

The First Custodian may still remember the world before the Dome.

---

# Core Philosophy

Elders represent power.
Scouts represent ambition.
Citizens represent society.

Custodians represent responsibility.

They are the ones who remain after conflict and rebuild the civilization.
