# Mobius HIVE — Biome Expansion Model v0.1

## Purpose

This document defines how HIVE expands beyond a single Dome into a broader civilization network.

The expansion model must preserve a core truth:

**HIVE is one civilization expressed through many worlds, not many disconnected worlds with shared branding.**

That means biome expansion should create variety without fragmenting identity, economy, or canon.

---

## Core thesis

A single Dome is the beginning, not the final form.

As HIVE matures, new environments should emerge that express different:
- material constraints
- aesthetics
- rituals
- strategic conditions
- learning contexts
- cultural skins
- gameplay emphases

But they must remain linked through one living civilizational framework.

One-line canon:

> **The HIVE grows by adding new worlds without forgetting the civilization that gave them meaning.**

---

## Expansion model

Biome expansion should happen in layers.

## Layer 1 — Primary Dome
This is the origin world.

Purpose:
- establish canon
- establish governance
- establish economy
- establish agent institutions
- establish memory infrastructure

The Primary Dome is the reference civilization shell.

---

## Layer 2 — Variant Domes
These are parallel biome expressions of the same civilization.

Examples:
- desert dome
- ocean dome
- frost dome
- forest dome
- volcanic dome
- orbital dome

Purpose:
- expand play style
- expand atmosphere
- expand strategic conditions
- test different civilizational pressures

---

## Layer 3 — Specialized Worlds
These are not just biome skins.
They are functionally specialized environments.

Examples:
- research-heavy dome
- education-first dome
- governance trial dome
- frontier extraction dome
- archive sanctuary dome
- athletic / competitive trial dome

Purpose:
- deepen specific systems
- create more concentrated civilization roles

---

## Layer 4 — Cultural Iterations
These are worlds that preserve HIVE structure while changing symbolic expression.

Examples:
- samurai-coded elder culture
- pharaoh-coded governance culture
- cyber-civic metropolis variant
- mythic forest republic variant
- orbital scholastic citadel variant

Purpose:
- allow cultural resonance and aesthetic breadth
- create multiple world expressions without breaking systemic continuity

---

## What must remain unified across all biomes

Biome variation is good.
Civilizational fragmentation is not.

The following should remain linked across all HIVE worlds:

### 1. Core economy
The same economic backbone should persist across worlds.

That means:
- one shared ledger root
- one shared underlying civic economy
- one continuity of ownership, stakes, records, and institutional meaning

A new biome should not feel like a hard reset of civilization identity.

---

### 2. Canon and memory continuity
The Archive logic must remain continuous.

A player’s acts in one biome should still matter in the wider civilization.

That means:
- one continuous memory layer
- inter-dome archive visibility
- monuments and historical records preserved across the network

---

### 3. Governance legitimacy framework
Different worlds can produce different political texture, but the deeper constitutional logic must remain recognizable.

That means:
- visible legitimacy
- challengeability
- office burden
- oath structure
- institutional memory of rule

---

### 4. Agent identity
AUREA, ZEUS, EVE, JADE, HERMES, ECHO, and ATLAS may take different visual forms in different biomes, but their deeper institutional roles should remain stable.

AUREA may become a desert observatory in one Dome and an orbital lattice in another, but she must still feel like AUREA.

## Biome expansion rules

## Rule 1 — each biome must change pressure, not just color palette
A biome should introduce meaningful civilizational difference.

Good expansion:
- different scarcity model
- different movement model
- different risk/reward balance
- different emotional climate

Bad expansion:
- same Dome, different wallpaper

---

## Rule 2 — every biome must still feel like HIVE
Even with aesthetic shifts, a biome must still preserve:
- visible public life
- ledger consequence
- institutional presence
- role-based contribution
- memory continuity

---

## Rule 3 — world identity must be readable quickly
A player should enter a biome and instantly feel:
- what kind of place this is
- what kind of survival / civic logic dominates here
- how this world differs from the Primary Dome

---

## Rule 4 — inter-dome travel should feel meaningful
Traveling between biomes should not feel like switching tabs.

It should feel like:
- expedition
- transfer
- diplomatic visit
- migration
- assignment
- pilgrimage

---

## Inter-dome systems

Biome expansion needs connective tissue.

## 1. Portal / transit network
Purpose:
- move players, goods, messages, and institutional signals

### Forms
- ring gates
- transit rails
- airships
- ocean lanes
- orbital lifts
- shard portals

---

## 2. Archive continuity layer
Purpose:
- preserve a shared civilization memory across all biomes

### Forms
- mirrored archive nodes
- central memory root
- inter-dome canon links
- monument registry

---

## 3. Shared governance layer
Purpose:
- keep the civilization politically linked

Possible models:
- local dome councils + central elder layer
- shard elders + civilization elder chamber
- dome-specific governance styles under one constitutional root

---

## 4. Shared economic layer
Purpose:
- one civilizational market and utility backbone

This avoids every dome becoming an isolated mini-game economy.

---

## Progression model for expansion

Biome expansion should be unlocked, not assumed.

### Phase 1
One Primary Dome

### Phase 2
One secondary biome dome

### Phase 3
Three-to-five biome variants with trade and archive linkage

### Phase 4
Specialized and cultural worlds

### Phase 5
Full HIVE civilization network

This allows growth without premature sprawl.

---

## Player meaning across biomes

A player should be able to say:
- this is my home Dome
- these are the worlds I have visited
- this is where my work mattered most
- this is where my structure or institution stands

That creates civilizational identity, not just map completion.

---

## Agent reskin rule

Agents may change visual expression by biome.

Examples:
- AUREA in desert biome = solar observatory
- AUREA in orbital biome = stellar lattice
- JADE in forest biome = living grove sanctuary
- ZEUS in volcanic biome = forge bastion
- HERMES in ocean biome = harbor relay tower

But the functional and mythic role must remain continuous.

---

## Anti-failure rules

Biome expansion must avoid becoming:
- endless map sprawl
- disconnected content islands
- cosmetic fragmentation
- lore drift through uncontrolled variation
- isolated economies with no civilizational continuity

### Guardrails
- every biome must add systemic difference
- all biomes must remain archive-linked
- identity should persist across worlds
- expansion should strengthen civilization, not dissolve it

---

## MVP recommendation

Do not build multiple biomes in the first playable HIVE version.

Instead:
- define the Primary Dome clearly
- design one secondary biome prototype on paper
- use that prototype to test whether HIVE can scale without losing identity

Best first secondary biome:
**Desert Dome** or **Orbital Dome**

Why:
- Desert tests scarcity and grounded trade
- Orbital tests high-level future civilization identity
