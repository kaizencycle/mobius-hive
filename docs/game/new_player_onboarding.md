# Mobius HIVE — New Player Onboarding v0.1

## Purpose

This document defines how a first-time player should enter HIVE without feeling lost, overwhelmed, or reduced to a generic tutorial subject.

The onboarding system should do more than teach controls.
It should teach the player what kind of civilization they are entering.

One-line canon:

> **Scout onboarding teaches the player how to move through HIVE. Citizen onboarding teaches the player how to live inside HIVE.**

---

## Core thesis

A new player should not arrive as a fully formed elite citizen.
They should begin as a **Scout**.

The Scout role gives them:
- room to explore
- room to learn
- room to prove themselves
- room to attach emotionally to the Dome

After early discovery and first meaningful contribution, the player should transition into **Citizen** status.

That transition matters because it tells the player:

**you are no longer just passing through the civilization — you now belong to it.**

---

## Onboarding phases

The onboarding model should happen in two main stages:

## 1. Scout onboarding
The player enters the world as a newcomer, explorer, and learner.

## 2. Citizen onboarding
The player gains a recognized civic foothold and begins participating in the social, economic, reflective, and governance life of the Dome.

These are not just progression tiers.
They are identity shifts.

---

# Stage 1 — Scout Onboarding

## Scout role definition

A Scout is:
- a first arrival
- an explorer of districts
- a gatherer of first experiences
- a seeker of orientation
- a learner who has not yet fully entered civic life

The Scout should feel mobile, curious, and slightly unanchored at first.
That is part of the narrative.

---

## Scout onboarding goals

By the end of Scout onboarding, the player should:
- know the basic shape of the Dome
- understand where the core districts are
- complete at least one small contribution loop
- experience the world reacting to them
- gain a first personal anchor in the civilization
- feel ready to become a Citizen

---

## Scout first-login flow

### 1. Entry
Player arrives through the Entry Screen and enters the Dome for the first time.

### 2. First spawn
Player appears in **Citizen Plaza**.

### 3. First guidance
A soft guidance layer explains:
- current cycle
- where they are
- what the major visible landmarks mean
- where to begin

### 4. First orientation quest
The player is asked to visit major landmarks and learn the basic geography.

### 5. First contribution
The player completes a small task that leaves a visible trace.

### 6. First recognition
The player receives acknowledgment that they are not just a visitor — they are now visible to the Dome.

---

## Scout tutorial principles

The Scout experience should avoid feeling like:
- a list of chores
- a forced exposition dump
- a generic RPG tutorial
- a lifeless software walkthrough

Instead it should feel like:
- arrival
- guided discovery
- first belonging
- first mark on the world

---

## Scout key onboarding tasks

## Task 1 — Read the Dome
The player learns the five core civilizational forces through movement:
- authority
- memory
- exchange
- reflection
- growth

This is taught by routing the Scout through visible landmarks.

### First landmarks to encounter
- Citizen Plaza
- Castle of Elders
- Archive Vault
- Market Quarter
- Reflection Gardens
- optionally School of AI / OAA

---

## Task 2 — Learn basic movement and inspect
The player learns:
- walk / navigate
- inspect a building
- open one overlay
- return from overlay back to the world

This is critical because HIVE depends on the player understanding the relationship between:
- place
- overlay
- action

---

## Task 3 — Perform one small reflection
The player visits Reflection Gardens and leaves a very small trace.

Example:
- a first reflection
- a first message to self
- a first intention marker

### World response
- a lantern lights
- a memory bloom appears
- a small acknowledgment is recorded

This teaches the player:
**inner life matters here.**

---

## Task 4 — Perform one small public or economic act
The player visits Market Quarter or another civic district and does one small action.

Examples:
- accept a basic exchange or starter task
- route a tiny resource
- claim a starter tool
- participate in a public utility action

This teaches the player:
**value moves through this world in visible ways.**

---

## Task 5 — Visit Archive and learn continuity
The player visits Archive Vault and sees that the civilization remembers.

They should understand:
- cycles exist
- memory exists
- world events persist
- they are entering something older than their arrival

This teaches the player:
**HIVE is already alive when you arrive.**

---

## Task 6 — Receive first personal anchor
At the end of Scout onboarding, the player should receive a first small spatial anchor.

Examples:
- field lodge
- Scout outpost
- starter chamber
- small dwelling

This should not be luxurious.
It should say:
**you have a place here now.**

---

## Scout completion condition

A Scout becomes ready for Citizen onboarding after:
- visiting core landmarks
- completing one reflection action
- completing one public or economic action
- receiving their first personal anchor
- understanding the basic shape of the Dome

This can be tracked by a simple Scout completion record.

---

## Scout emotional outcome

By the end of Scout onboarding, the player should feel:
- less lost
- more curious
- lightly recognized
- emotionally attached to the Dome
- ready for deeper belonging

Canon sentence:

**A Scout should leave onboarding feeling that they have entered a living civilization and that the civilization has noticed them in return.**

---

# Stage 2 — Citizen Onboarding

## Citizen role definition

A Citizen is:
- a recognized participant in the Dome
- a member of the civic body
- someone who can contribute more meaningfully
- someone whose actions now have more durable consequence

Where the Scout learns movement, the Citizen learns belonging.

---

## Citizen onboarding goals

By the end of Citizen onboarding, the player should:
- understand their civic role
- have a recognized place in the world
- complete at least one meaningful civic loop
- understand that they can shape the civilization
- unlock deeper systems like governance-adjacent participation, festivals, or more durable contribution paths

---

## Citizen onboarding flow

## Step 1 — Formal recognition
The player is acknowledged as a Citizen.

This should feel ceremonial, even if brief.

Possible forms:
- Citizen Plaza recognition event
- oath prompt
- archive registration
- a visible change to their personal structure

This moment matters because the role changes from explorer to participant.

---

## Step 2 — Citizen place assignment
The player’s personal anchor evolves into a more civic identity.

Examples:
- upgraded chamber
- recognized dwelling
- neighborhood or district-linked home
- workshop or role-specific room

This says:
**you now live here, not just pass through here.**

---

## Step 3 — First meaningful civic loop
The Citizen must complete one meaningful act that connects them to the public life of the Dome.

Examples:
- complete a market or public-goods task
- contribute to repair or upkeep
- complete a learning loop with visible effect
- place a first entry in shared memory
- support a civic event or public need

This teaches the player:
**your contribution can now shape the world, not just observe it.**

---

## Step 4 — Learn the civic systems
The Citizen is introduced to the systems they may later participate in more deeply.

These include:
- economy / exchange
- memory / archive
- reflection
- festivals
- governance-adjacent activity
- future oath systems

This does not mean unlocking everything at once.
It means showing the player what kind of life is possible inside HIVE.

---

## Step 5 — First public memory
The player should produce or participate in one act that enters the more public memory of the Dome.

Examples:
- a named contribution
- a visible repair
- a public festival participation marker
- a teaching or support act

This is the moment where the player starts to matter historically, even if on a small scale.

---

## Citizen completion condition

A Citizen onboarding path is considered complete when the player has:
- been formally recognized
- received a civic place
- completed one meaningful civic act
- seen the world respond
- understood where deeper systems will take them next

At that point, the player is no longer in tutorial space.
They are inside the civilization proper.

---

## Citizen emotional outcome

By the end of Citizen onboarding, the player should feel:
- settled
- recognized
- responsible
- visible
- motivated to return

Canon sentence:

**A Citizen should feel that they now have a place, a role, and a future inside the Dome.**

---

# Scout → Citizen transition

This transition should be explicit and meaningful.

It should not be just:
- level 2
- unlocked badge
- invisible backend flag

It should feel like:
- a rite of entry
- a civic adoption
- a recognition by the world

Possible transition elements:
- brief oath or declaration
- archive registration
- visible building upgrade
- district announcement
- new permissions and social affordances

---

## Core distinction

### Scout
- explores
- discovers
- learns the map
- proves first value
- receives first anchor

### Citizen
- belongs
- contributes
- sustains
- enters public memory
- becomes part of the civic body

Simple canon line:

**The Scout asks, “What is this world?” The Citizen asks, “What will I do for this world?”**

---

# UX design rules

## Rule 1
Never drown a new player in systems language.
Teach by place, action, and response.

## Rule 2
Early onboarding should teach the shape of the civilization, not just controls.

## Rule 3
The player should leave a visible trace before onboarding ends.

## Rule 4
The transition to Citizen must feel like belonging, not just progression.

## Rule 5
Onboarding should create emotional attachment before heavy system complexity.

---

# Recommended MVP implementation

For the first playable version, Scout and Citizen onboarding can be lightweight.

## Scout MVP
- Plaza spawn
- landmark visit path
- one reflection action
- one public/economic action
- starter chamber unlock

## Citizen MVP
- recognition moment
- chamber upgrade or civic place assignment
- one meaningful civic task
- one public memory trace
- introduction to governance/festival horizon

This is enough to establish the emotional structure of the onboarding system.

---

## Anti-failure rules

The onboarding system must avoid becoming:
- a generic fantasy tutorial
- a giant lore dump
- a spreadsheet checklist
- a detached UX flow with no world consequence
- a grind gate before the “real game” starts

### Guardrails
- every onboarding step should be tied to a place
- every major onboarding action should have visible response
- every new player should gain a real anchor in the world
- belonging should be felt, not just labeled

---

## Canon sentences

**Scout onboarding teaches the player how to move through HIVE. Citizen onboarding teaches the player how to live inside HIVE.**

**The onboarding journey should move the player from first contact, to first trace, to first belonging.**
