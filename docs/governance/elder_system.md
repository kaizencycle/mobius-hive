# Mobius HIVE — Elder System v0.1

## Purpose

This document defines the Elder system as the constitutional scarcity layer of HIVE.

Elders are not cosmetic rulers.
They are visible civic offices with real legitimacy, real public burden, and real risk of challenge.

The Elder system should make governance feel:
- scarce
- embodied
- contestable
- ritualized
- publicly legible

---

## Core thesis

A throne should not mean unlimited power.
A throne should mean visible responsibility under continuous public legitimacy pressure.

One-line canon:

> **The burden is not to sit, but to hold.**

---

## Hard scarcity rule

There are only **seven thrones**.

One throne for each shard or civic pillar.
No matter how many players are strong, famous, wealthy, or skilled, only seven Elders may sit at one time.

This creates:
- prestige
- competition
- narrative gravity
- constitutional scarcity

### Example throne pillars
- Ash
- Veils
- Frost
- Song
- Stone
- Echoes
- Dawn

These may later be reskinned or renamed by season, but the hard cap remains.

---

## Elder seat model

Each Elder seat has:
- a throne
- a term
- a legitimacy score
- a visible district effect
- a challenge condition
- a citizen oath relationship

An Elder is not just a rank.
An Elder is a **seat-bound office**.

That means:
- the office matters more than the individual
- the individual gives the office style, strategy, and symbolic direction
- the public can distinguish the throne from the current holder

---

## Paths to Elder status

## 1. Empty-seat ascension
If a throne is empty, a qualified Crown-Bearer may attempt ascension.

### Requirements
- Crown assembled or equivalent progression threshold met
- required burn / bond paid
- public support threshold met
- completion of the Ascension Rite / Trial of Mirrors

### Outcome
- success → seat claimed
- failure → bond burned and cooldown applied

---

## 2. Challenge a seated Elder
If all seats are occupied, a new contender must challenge a current Elder.

### Requirements
- challenger qualifies for challenge tier
- challenge bond posted
- citizen backing threshold reached
- scheduled trial window opens

### Outcome
- win → current Elder unseated, challenger ascends
- lose → bond burned, cooldown triggered, Elder legitimacy reinforced

---

## 3. Retirement / vacancy succession
An Elder may retire, be removed, or lose the seat through inactivity or governance failure.

### Outcome
- seat becomes vacant
- ascension window opens
- public notices update

---

## Elder term model

Elders do not sit forever.

### Default term
- **90 real days** per season

A term may end by:
- natural expiration
- successful challenge
- voluntary retirement
- legitimacy collapse
- inactivity enforcement

This keeps the Castle alive and prevents frozen aristocracy.

---

## Legitimacy model

Every Elder has a **legitimacy state** that is visible to the civilization.

### Inputs to legitimacy
- citizen oath support
- proposal outcomes
- district stewardship
- event performance
- challenge success
- inactivity / neglect penalties
- scandal or constitutional breach

### Legitimacy bands
#### Stable
- banners bright
- decrees carry full force
- district effects are strong

#### Strained
- visual cracks or dimming begin
- public doubt appears
- decrees may weaken

#### Fragile
- challenge vulnerability rises
- district confidence drops
- throne environment visibly destabilizes

#### Broken
- throne is in crisis
- special challenge or removal conditions unlock

---

## Citizen oath power

Citizens are not passive spectators.
They are part of the force that keeps or breaks a throne.

### Oath mechanics
Citizens may:
- support an Elder
- oppose an Elder
- remain neutral
- commit stake to governance rituals

### Effects
- support strengthens throne legitimacy
- opposition weakens throne legitimacy faster
- neutrality may preserve optionality but does not stabilize rule

### Important rule
Citizen support must cost something real.
Otherwise governance becomes fake applause.

Examples of cost:
- staked governance token
- burned ritual amount
- locked civic resource

---

## Decree model

Elders may issue decrees, but decrees should not function as unchecked commands.

### Decree flow
1. Elder proposes decree
2. decree enters public or council review window
3. citizen or institutional response occurs
4. decree resolves into:
   - passed
   - failed
   - contested
   - deferred

### Rule
The throne gives voice and agenda-setting power.
It does not eliminate the civilization’s right to respond.

---

## Challenge system

Challenges should be theatrical, rare, and meaningful.

### Challenge structure
- challenger registration
- bond posting
- public backing phase
- ritual announcement
- trial execution
- result recording in Archive and Castle state

### Trial types
#### Trial of Mirrors
Measures integrity, self-consistency, and symbolic worthiness.

#### Trial of Deeds
Measures contribution, competence, and public record.

#### Trial of Echoes
Measures citizen resonance, support, and civic recognition.

A season may use one or a combination of these.

---

## Elder district effects

Every seated Elder should change the world around them.

### Examples
- Market-aligned Elder boosts exchange district bonuses
- Reflection-aligned Elder strengthens morale / memory districts
- Defense-aligned Elder increases resilience and warning visibility
- Learning-aligned Elder boosts school incentives and challenge unlocks

This makes rule visible and material, not just symbolic.

---

## Unseating effects

When an Elder falls, the world should visibly respond.

### World reactions
- throne light extinguishes
- banners shift or collapse
- public notices update
- challenge history enters Archive
- district effect weakens or changes hands

This moment should feel major.

---

## Retirement model

A retired Elder should not vanish into irrelevance.

### Post-seat state
- retired Elders become **Spectral Teachers**, advisors, or honored former rulers
- they keep symbolic prestige
- they lose active decree power
- some halls or monuments may preserve their era

This allows continuity without permanent rule.

---

## Anti-failure rules

The Elder system must avoid becoming:
- static monarchy
- empty leaderboard aristocracy
- pay-to-rule structure
- pure popularity contest
- lore theater disconnected from mechanics

### Guardrails
- hard seat scarcity
- real challenge path
- visible legitimacy pressure
- public cost for governance influence
- term expiration
- institutional memory of past rule

---

## MVP recommendation

For the first playable HIVE build, implement a lighter Elder system:

### MVP Elder features
- seven visible empty or occupied thrones
- legitimacy indicator per throne
- one active challenge path
- one oath support / oppose mechanic
- 90-day seasonal timer
- archive record of current and former holders

This is enough to make the Castle feel alive without overbuilding the entire governance engine at once.
