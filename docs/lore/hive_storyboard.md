# HIVE — Civilization Storyboard Canon

## Purpose

This document defines the narrative arc structure of HIVE as a civilization-scale world.

HIVE is not intended to function as a traditional linear game.

It is:
- a living civilization shell
- a governance simulation
- a ritual economy
- a memory-preservation system
- and a world where meaning itself becomes scarce.

One-line canon:

> HIVE begins as a game and evolves into a civilization capable of remembering itself.

---

# Core Story Arc

## Act I — The First Reflection

A lone Scout enters the Dome.

The civilization appears dormant.
Most Citizens no longer remember why the Ledger exists.
The Castle still stands, but many halls are empty.

The Scout discovers a fractured shard.

JADE explains:

> Every shard contains memory.

The first Festival of Echoes begins.
Citizens burn GIC not for profit, but for legitimacy.
The Dome reacts.

The player realizes:

The civilization is alive.

---

## Act II — The Castle of Elders

The Seven Thrones are revealed.

Each throne is tied to a shard-domain:
- Ash
- Veils
- Frost
- Song
- Stone
- Echoes
- Dawn

Crown-Bearers emerge.
Citizens divide into ideological factions.

The first Elder passes an edict.
Citizens resist through Oath Pools.

The player learns:

Governance in HIVE is ritualized economic pressure.

---

## Act III — Trial of Mirrors

A challenger enters the Trial.

The Dome generates living reflections.
Players confront:
- fear
- memory
- ambition
- sacrifice
- identity drift

Failure burns:
- GIC
- legitimacy
- fragments of memory continuity.

Victory grants ascension eligibility.

EVE reveals:

> The Castle is not governance.
> The Castle is memory made permanent.

---

## Act IV — The Fracture

A biome diverges from canonical continuity.

A cyberpunk mirror-Dome appears.
A faction attempts permanent throne occupation.

Reality destabilizes.

The Ledger detects imbalance.
Festival burn pressure rises.
Citizens must choose:
- freedom
- stability
- continuity
- expansion.

The HIVE fractures into competing realities.

---

## Act V — Civilization Protocol

Multiple Domes now coexist.

Examples:
- orbital colonies
- ocean civilizations
- desert biomes
- educational HIVE systems
- cultural mirror variants.

All worlds remain tied to one GIC ledger.

Meaning becomes the scarce resource.

History itself becomes gameplay.

---

## Act VI — The Final Echo

An Elder voluntarily steps down.

A Spectral Teacher emerges.

Citizens begin self-governance without dependence on rulers.
The Dome stabilizes.

The civilization no longer survives because of power.

It survives because it remembers.

---
